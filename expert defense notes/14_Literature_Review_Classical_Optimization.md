# Literature Review: Classical Optimization

## Purpose of this subsection
This file helps you answer questions specifically about the classical-finance side of the literature review.

## Core message
Classical optimization created the language of diversification, but real-world instability made that framework insufficient on its own.

## Markowitz (1952)
### What to say
- Markowitz introduced mean-variance optimization and the efficient frontier.
- It is the intellectual foundation of modern portfolio theory.

### Why it appears in your dissertation
- Because your work is reacting to the limits of classical diversification, not ignoring its importance.

### Best viva answer
- "Markowitz gave the first formal mathematical diversification framework. My dissertation starts there because the whole project is motivated by where that framework becomes fragile."

## Why MVO is still important even if it fails in practice
- It remains the benchmark for optimization-based diversification.
- It teaches the language of risk-return trade-off.
- It remains a standard baseline in academic and industry discussion.

## Michaud (1989)
### Why this paper matters
- It explains that optimized portfolios can be very sensitive to noisy inputs.
- The optimizer may magnify estimation errors.

### Best answer
- "Michaud is essential because he distinguishes mathematical optimality from empirical reliability."

## Ledoit and Wolf (2004)
### Why included
- Shows that researchers tried to fix covariance estimation problems rather than abandon MVO immediately.

### What that helps you argue
- Your dissertation is not rejecting classical methods casually.
- It is positioned after serious attempts to stabilize them.

## DeMiguel, Garlappi, and Uppal (2009)
### Why it is central
- It is one of the strongest empirical attacks on complex portfolio methods.
- It shows how hard it is to beat equal-weight out of sample.

### Best answer
- "This paper is central because it turns the problem from theory into a strong empirical challenge. If a complex model cannot beat 1/N, the complexity may not be justified."

## Chua et al., Ang and Chen, Flint et al.
### Why these matter
- They support the claim that diversification weakens exactly when stress is highest.
- They justify your focus on drawdown and crisis robustness.

### Best answer
- "These papers support the idea that correlation is state-dependent. Apparent diversification in normal periods can vanish in turbulent regimes."

## Why this literature alone does not solve your problem
- Classical methods still depend heavily on price-derived inputs.
- They remain backward-looking.
- They do not explicitly encode business similarity.

## Why not solve this just by better covariance estimation
Best answer:
- "Better covariance estimation helps numerical stability, but it does not solve price blindness. My dissertation's claim is that the missing issue is informational, not only statistical."

## Final one-line summary
- "Classical optimization gave the right question, but not always the right information source."
