# Glossary and Key Terms

## Ablation study
A structured experiment where one component is changed or removed to see how much it matters.

## Alpha
Return above a benchmark or above what is expected for the amount of risk taken.

## Baseline
A comparison method used to judge whether the proposed model adds value.

## Beta
A measure of how sensitive an asset is to the market.

## Capital allocation
The process of deciding how much money goes into different parts of the portfolio.

## Cash asset
A zero-risk or low-risk placeholder allocation used to avoid forced investment.

## Cosine similarity
A measure of how similar two vectors are in direction.

## Credit assignment
The problem of deciding which agent or action deserves credit or blame for the final outcome.

## Curriculum learning
A training strategy where learning is organized in stages from simpler to more complex settings.

## CVaR
Conditional Value at Risk. Average loss in the worst tail of the return distribution.

## Dirichlet distribution
A probability distribution defined on non-negative vectors that sum to one. Very suitable for portfolio weights.

## Drawdown
The decline from a peak portfolio value to a later trough.

## EIIE
Ensemble of Identical Independent Evaluators. A portfolio-network design using shared filters across assets.

## Effective N
An interpretable version of concentration. It tells you how many equally weighted assets would give the same HHI.

## Entropy bonus
An RL training term that encourages exploration and prevents early collapse to deterministic behavior.

## HHI
Herfindahl-Hirschman Index. A concentration measure based on squared weights.

## Holdout set
A final unseen test period reserved for honest evaluation.

## Lexical matrix
The matrix of pairwise business-description similarities across firms.

## Log return
The natural logarithm of portfolio gross return. Often used in finance because of compounding properties.

## MARL
Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning.

## Maximum drawdown
The worst peak-to-trough portfolio loss over a period.

## Mean-variance optimization
The classical Markowitz framework that trades expected return against variance.

## Non-stationarity
A condition where the environment or target changes during learning.

## Null hypothesis
The default scientific assumption being tested. Here, that semantic identity does not matter beyond generic regularization.

## Out of sample
Test data that the model did not use during training.

## Portfolio Vector Memory
The previous portfolio weights fed back into the model as part of the state.

## Price blindness
The weakness of using only price data while ignoring business fundamentals.

## REINFORCE
A basic policy-gradient reinforcement learning algorithm.

## Regime
A market environment with its own characteristic behavior, such as bull, crisis, or recovery.

## Reward shaping
Designing the reward function to guide the learning behavior toward desired properties.

## Risk parity
A portfolio strategy that equalizes risk contribution rather than capital weight.

## Sharpe ratio
Return per unit of total volatility.

## Simplex
The space of non-negative vectors whose entries sum to one.

## Softmax
A function that maps scores into normalized positive weights. Common, but not always ideal for portfolio allocation.

## Sortino ratio
Return per unit of downside volatility.

## Staged training
A curriculum where workers and manager are trained in separate phases before joint fine-tuning.

## Structural diversification
Diversification based on meaningful economic difference, not only historical price correlation.

## Tail dependence
The tendency of assets to become more correlated in extreme downside states.

## TF-IDF
Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency. A standard way to represent text using informative word weighting.

## Turnover
The amount of change in the portfolio weights from one period to the next.

## Walk-forward validation
A time-series evaluation method where training uses earlier data and testing uses later unseen data.

## WALP
Weight-Aware Lexical Penalty. The semantic diversification penalty used in the dissertation.

## w transpose S w
The quadratic form used by WALP. It combines portfolio weights and business similarity in one penalty term.
